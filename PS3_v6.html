This is the highest marks module. RRRV derivation alone is worth 6–10 marks every year. Master it first. Then CB ratings numerical (2025 had a full 5-mark sum).
  • Derivations needed: RRRV expression, CB ratings formulas (making current = 2.55 × Is, symmetrical breaking current).
  • Diagrams needed: HRC fuse cut-off characteristics, SF6 CB, Vacuum CB, arc voltage waveform.
  • Start with PYQs from 2022 and 2024 to understand what depth is expected.
  • Notes strategy: 2 pages — one for fuses (all terms), one for CB comparison table + RRRV derivation steps.
🔹 B. Common Mistakes
  • In RRRV: confusing peak restriking voltage with maximum RRRV — they are different formulas.
  • Current chopping: forgetting to mention its harmful effect (over-voltage on CB and system).
  • HRC fuse: not drawing the cut-off characteristics diagram when asked.
  • CB ratings: confusing symmetrical breaking current with making current — know both formulas.
  • SF6 vs Vacuum CB: writing wrong advantages (e.g., saying SF6 needs no maintenance — it does require gas monitoring).
  • Not mentioning the arc quenching medium for each CB type.
🔹 C. Must-Know Concepts
  • RRRV = ω × Vm × √(L/C)  |  Max RRRV = ω² × Vm × √(L/C)
  • Making current = 2.55 × symmetrical breaking current (RMS)
  • Short circuit MVA = √3 × kV × symmetrical breaking current (kA)
  • All fuse terms: fusing factor, minimum fusing current, pre-arcing time, arcing time, cut-off current, prospective current.
  • I = k × d3/2 relationship for fuses (wire diameter vs fusing current).
  • HRC fuse construction: ceramic body, silver element, quartz sand filler, end caps.
  • Arc extinction theories: high resistance theory, low resistance (current zero) theory.
  • SF6 properties: electronegative, non-toxic, non-flammable, excellent dielectric.

M4 Module 4 — Protective Relays  |  HIGH Priority  |  ⏱ 10–12 hrs
🔹 A. Study Approach
1
Concept
2

PYQs
Distance relay question (12 marks in 2024) is the single highest-value question in this module. Master R-X diagrams for impedance, reactance, mho relay. Alternator protection + Numerical relay are the next priorities.
  • Start with distance relays — learn R-X diagram plotting for all 3 types.
  • Alternator protection: memorize the 4 types asked repeatedly (overvoltage, loss of excitation, failure of prime mover, unbalanced loading).
  • Numerical relay: block diagram + differences from static relay (asked as full Q in 2025).
  • Notes strategy: R-X diagram sheet + alternator protection summary table.
🔹 B. Common Mistakes
  • Distance relay R-X diagrams: drawing incorrect circles/lines — impedance relay is a circle centered at origin, mho is a circle passing through origin, reactance is a horizontal line.
  • PSM calculation: forgetting to divide by CT ratio first, then plug setting percentage.
  • TMS (Time Multiplier Setting) confused with PSM (Plug Setting Multiplier) — know both definitions.
  • Alternator protection: confusing "loss of excitation" with "under-voltage" — they are different.
  • Numerical relay: not drawing the block diagram when asked.
🔹 C. Must-Know Concepts
  • PSM = Fault current through relay / Relay current setting (= Fault current / (CT ratio × plug setting %)).
  • Zone of protection concept: Zone 1 (80–85% of line), Zone 2 (120–150%), Zone 3 (200–250%).
  • Impedance relay: circle on R-X plane centered at origin — operates when |Z| is less than set value.
  • Mho relay: circle passing through origin, directional, best for long lines.
  • Reactance relay: horizontal line, not directional, best for short lines.
  • Universal torque equation: T = K1I² + K2V² + K3VI cos(δ–τ) + K4.
  • Numerical relay block diagram: Input → Analog filter → ADC → Microprocessor → Output to CB.
  • Buchholz relay: detects incipient faults in oil-immersed transformers.

Attenuation of travelling waves (analytical derivation) has appeared in 2023 and 2024 for 5 marks each — learn to write it. Lightning phenomenon diagrams (stepped leader, dart leader) are 4–5 marks and easy if you draw + label properly.
  • Analytically derive exponential attenuation of V and I along a line — this is repeatedly asked.
  • Lightning phenomenon: 4 stages to know — pilot streamer, stepped leader, dart leader, cold lightning stroke.
  • Ground wire protection: angle of protection, two configurations (single wire, double wire).
  • Notes strategy: one page for lightning stages + ground wire configurations; one page for insulation coordination terms.
🔹 B. Common Mistakes
  • Attenuation derivation: using wrong signs in the differential equations for V and I.
  • Confusing BIL (Basic Impulse Insulation Level) with flashover voltage — BIL is the withstand level, not flashover.
  • Lightning arrester vs surge absorber: different devices — arrester limits voltage magnitude, absorber limits rate of rise.
  • Not drawing diagrams for ground wire configurations (sheltering angle).
  • Insulation coordination: forgetting to state that BIL of equipment must be greater than the protective level of arrester.
🔹 C. Must-Know Concepts
  • Velocity of travelling wave = 1/√(LC) = speed of light (3×10⁸ m/s) for overhead lines.
  • Attenuation: V = V₀ e^(−αx), I = I₀ e^(−αx); cause of attenuation — corona loss and leakage.
  • Surge impedance = √(L/C) ≈ 400Ω (overhead) / 50Ω (cable).
  • BIL = voltage level (peak) that equipment insulation can withstand for a standard 1.2/50 μs impulse.
  • Dry FOV < Wet FOV (wet flashover is at lower voltage because water layer increases conduction).
  • Valve-type LA: silicon carbide non-linear resistor in series with spark gaps.
  • Surge absorber: high inductance + capacitance to absorb fast-front surges.

M3 Module 3 — Fuses & Circuit Breakers  |  HIGHEST PRIORITY
⚡ M3 contributes ~20–25 marks every single year. It has the most PYQ coverage of any module. Never skip this module.
TopicFreqStatusWhat exactly to study / what to skip

Air Blast CB / Oil CB / MOCB 0–1/4 SKIP Never asked as standalone long question. Know only for the CB comparison table (1 row each). Do NOT write notes on these individually.

M5 Module 5 — Overvoltage & Insulation Coordination  |  HIGH PRIORITY
⚡ M5 has the most syllabus content but questions are very focused. The same 4–5 topics repeat. Don't waste time on the theoretical overvoltage generation mechanisms — focus on protection.
TopicFreqStatusWhat exactly to study / what to skip
Lightning Phenomenon 4/4 ✅ MUST Mechanism: cloud charging → pilot streamer → stepped leader (downward) → dart leader → return stroke (upward). Hot stroke vs cold stroke. Draw figure showing all stages. Asked 3/4 years in long answers + MCQs.
Valve-type Lightning Arrester 3/4 MUST Construction: spark gaps + SiC non-linear resistors in series, porcelain housing. Switching Surges (theory) 0/4 SKIP Never appeared as a direct question. Know the term definition only (overvoltage due to switching operations). Do not prepare notes.
Ferranti Effect as overvoltage source 0/4 SKIP Not asked in any year. Skip.

M1 is conceptual only — no derivations, no diagrams, no numericals. The Deregulation + Electricity Act 2003 question has appeared in almost the same wording 4 years in a row. One focused hour covers this entire module.
TopicFreqStatusWhat exactly to study / what to skip
Deregulation — definition, reasons, Indian scenario 4/4 ✅ MUST Definition: opening the electric power industry to competition by separating generation, transmission, and distribution. Reasons: monopoly inefficiency, high tariffs, lack of investment. Indian scenario: pre-1991 monolithic SEBs → post-liberalisation → Electricity Act 2003. Write 1 page answering this and it covers 3/4 years' questions.
Electricity Act 2003 — key provisions 4/4 ✅ MUST Key points: (1) Unbundling of Generation/Transmission/Distribution, (2) Open access to transmission network, (3) Delicensing of generation, (4) Creation of SERCs and CERC, (5) Competition in retail supply. Always asked together with deregulation. Memorise these 5 points.
Entities in restructured environment 3/4 MUST 6 entities: GENCO (generation company), TRANSCO (transmission), DISCO (distribution), ESCO (energy service company), ISO (Independent System Operator), RTO (Regional Transmission Operator). Know role of each in one sentence. Asked as fill-in-blanks in 2022 and 2023.
Bulk Power Grid vs Micro Power Grid 2/4 STUDY Bulk: large-scale, centralized, national/regional grid, high voltage transmission. Micro: small-scale, local community, uses DG/solar/wind/storage, can island. Key difference: islanding capability and source type. The comparison table in Section 3 is sufficient prep.
Power System Structure (generation → transmission → distribution) 1/4 SKIM General overview: generating station → step-up transformer → EHV transmission → step-down → primary distribution → secondary distribution → consumer. Know voltage levels (11kV, 33kV, 110kV, 220kV, 400kV). 5 minutes max.
Regulated vs Deregulated industry comparison 2/4 STUDY Table: regulated (government monopoly, fixed tariff, vertically integrated, no consumer choice) vs deregulated (open market, market-driven pricing, independent producers, consumer can choose supplier). The comparison table in Section 3 is ready-to-use.
Electricity Market Models 1/4 SKIM 2024 Q2(b) asked specifically about "electricity market entities and model." Pool model vs bilateral contracts model. Short note level only — one paragraph is sufficient.
